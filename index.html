Jill Scott: Poet, Singer, Mother

Photo by Justin de Nooijer

Jill Scott was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, in 1972. She is a neo soul singer-songwriter, poet and actress active since 1999. Her debut album, “Who is Jill Scott? Words and Sounds Vol. 1” went platinum in the U.S., and her later albums (“The Light of the Sun” and “Woman”) reached the number one position on the Billboard 200 chart.

Scott’s first film appearance was in the 2007 Deborah Kampmeier film “Hounddog,” where she played Big Mama Thornton. In 2008, she played “Shiela” in the Tyler Perry cult classic “Why Did I Get Married?”

Scott’s musical career has been embellished with accolades, including three Grammys, three Soul Train Music Awards and four Lady of Soul Awards. Her delivery is poetic, rife with intimate lyrics and jazzy rhythm. Her songs typically center around her experience as a woman, a mother and a lover.
